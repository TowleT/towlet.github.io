I   The Book

What Chimera is about.

Literary Science Fiction

Chimera:
The Hypatia Chronicle

Thomas E. Towle

Oxford Bean Publishing · 2026 ISBN 978-0-9812955-6-5 Literary Science Fiction · Novel

In a post-Carbon Wars world of collapsed nations and resurgent theocracy, a fossil hunter named Luchs lies in an induced coma, held by a ruthless religious government called the Precisians. They want one thing: the location of an extraordinary prehistoric fossil — the Chimera — buried somewhere in the Shining Mountains. Luchs alone knows where it is. Even unconscious, he resists.

Into this standoff comes a monk-historian, a mountain rebel leader, an ancient AI called Bot, and the defining love of Luchs's life — Hypatia, a scientist being consumed from within by something she calls the Disease Voice. A presence that has learned to speak in her own cadence. A presence that may not be illness at all.

At its core, Chimera is about resistance and the limits of love — about who controls the past and therefore owns the future, and about what it means to build a true picture of the world when the world itself is being rewritten around you.

"The fossil and I have been waiting the same amount of time. Only one of us knows what for." — Bot, in Chimera: The Hypatia Chronicle

II   The Reader

Who it's for.

📚

Literary fiction readers

If you love Ursula K. Le Guin's moral weight, Kazuo Ishiguro's interiority, or the philosophical restlessness of Doris Lessing — this is for you.

🚀

Science fiction readers

Dystopian world-building, a post-collapse political landscape, an ancient AI with its own agenda, and a mystery at the edge of human evolutionary history.

🏔

Outdoor & nature writers

The mountains are not backdrop — they are presence. Readers of Edward Abbey, John Muir, or Barry Lopez will recognise the landscape as character.

🧬

Science & ideas readers

The fossil at the heart of the novel raises serious questions about evolutionary biology, exogenesis, and what humanity might carry inside it without knowing.

✍️

Book bloggers & reviewers

If you cover independent or literary fiction, debut novels, or speculative fiction from outside the mainstream — we would be honoured to be on your list.

🎙

Podcast & radio hosts

Thomas is available for interviews, conversations, and guest appearances. The author, the book, and the 1987 Toyota motorhome he writes from are all part of the story.
